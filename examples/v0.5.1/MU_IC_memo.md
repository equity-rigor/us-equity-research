## 3. INVESTMENT THESIS

**The debate is not about gross margin — it is about whether AI permanently resets Micron's revenue base.** This is the single most important reframing in the memo, and it is the one place a differentiated analyst can add evidenced edge (see §4, §6).

- **Bull (structural regime change):** HBM converts commodity memory into a supply-disciplined, sold-out, quasi-logic business. HBM is sold out through 2026 (price + volume **locked** via multiyear Strategic Customer Agreements), Micron can fill only **50–66%** of customer demand, and the 3-player oligopoly (Micron, SK Hynix, Samsung) is investing with discipline. If AI permanently lifts Micron's through-cycle revenue base toward $90–110B, normalized EPS is $25–37 (Wells Fargo's through-cycle $30–40) and the stock is not expensive.
- **Bear (peak-cyclical value trap):** Memory has *always* mean-reverted. Micron's GM went **60.6%→27% in three quarters (FY18→19)** and **45.2%→−9.1% (FY22→23, net loss)**. A record **>$25B FY26 capex** (facility-led) seeds a **2H'27–FY28 supply wave**, while a **+$7B depreciation step-up** lands in COGS by FY28–29 *regardless of pricing*. At a $50B mid-cycle revenue base, normalized EPS is only **~$11–13**, implying a fair value far below the current price.
- **Our view:** The bull case is **directionally consensus and locked for 2026**, but the price already embeds a structural re-rate (avg sell-side PT $739 / median $575 both sit *below* spot $864). The distribution is **negatively skewed** on a 12-month horizon. We are sellers of the outright and prefer the relative-value pair.

**Anchor source-levels:** Financials S1 (SEC XBRL/10-Q/8-K, verified). HBM "sold out 2026" S1/S2 (mgmt). HBM 2027 visibility **S2-soft** (frameworks, not proven take-or-pay — labeled conditional per G7). Cycle base-rates S1 (historical 8-Ks). Consensus/PTs S2. Supply-timing/China S2–S3.

**GM bridge — decomposing the ~81% peak (T1 → T4):**

| Component | GM pts | Persistence |
|---|---|---|
| Cyclical ASP premium (DRAM ASP +54%, supply-starved) | ~30–33 | **Mean-reverts fully** |

**The depreciation cliff:** >$25B FY26 + ~$30B FY27 capex converts to D&A on a ~6-yr schedule → D&A rises from **$8.35B (FY25) to ~$15–16B (FY28–29), a +$7B step = ~7–9 pts of GM drag that lands regardless of price**, precisely as the ASP supercycle fades.

**Net verdict:** Normalized mid-cycle **GM ~44–45%, first reached FY28–29** — *above* the old ~40% pre-HBM trough frame (HBM lifts it ~4 pts) but well *below* any "structural 50s" bull case (the cliff caps it ~7 pts). **The depreciation cliff modestly outweighs the HBM mix benefit on margin.**

**Normalized EPS — and why revenue, not GM, is the real debate:**
- At **$50B** mid-cycle revenue, 44.5% GM, $6B opex, 14.5% tax, 1,140M sh → **EPS ~$12** (range $11–13).
- At **$90B** (AI partial reset) → **~$25 EPS**. At **$110B / 50% GM** (Wells Fargo bull mix) → **~$37**.
- **The entire bull/bear EPS gap is the revenue-base assumption, not the margin.** GM is the symptom; *revenue durability is the disease.* This is the question to underwrite.
