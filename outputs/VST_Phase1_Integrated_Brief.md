## 2. The setup in one paragraph
VST is the largest US merchant power producer + retailer (~41 GW today → ~46–47 GW peak post-Cogentrix 2026 → ~43–45 GW 2028 after a −4.6 GW coal cliff), mid-to-late-cycle in an AI-power upcycle where PJM capacity has cleared at the FERC price cap two consecutive auctions. It has de-risked historically volatile merchant cash flow via ~3,809 MW of 20-year investment-grade nuclear PPAs (AWS, Meta) and a rolling hedge covering ~100%/89%/58% of 2026/27/28 generation. GAAP net income is non-informational (ASC 815 hedge mark-to-market); underwrite on Ongoing Adj EBITDA (FY25 $5.91B; 2026 guide $6.8–7.6B; 2027 opportunity $7.4–7.8B) and FCFbG ($3.925–4.725B 2026). Balance sheet is "crossover" IG (BBB− at S&P + Fitch; Moody's still Ba1), net leverage 2.78× FY25. The stock is ~34% off its 2024 high on **multiple compression, not estimate cuts**, and is a well-owned/crowded long mid-unwind.

## 3. Thesis tree (preliminary weights — Phase 1)
- **H1 — AI-power re-rating (35%).** Contracted IG PPAs + capacity-at-cap + FERC blessing of front-of-meter co-location + nuclear PTC floor de-risk EBITDA; ~8.6% FCF yield funds a buyback retiring ~3.5–4%/yr. *Falsifier:* PJM/ERCOT capacity or forward power prices roll over for 2+ quarters with no new PPAs.
- **H2 — commodity quality-trap already priced (40%).** Merchant cyclical narrated as secular; upside is regionally narrow (EIA: ERCOT-North spike is a high-demand-only scenario, PJM same scenario ≈ +4%); crowded long still unwinding; PTs being cut. *Falsifier:* a guidance raise + multiple stabilization at >10× fwd EV/EBITDA.
- **H3 — regulatory/structural tail (25%).** Hedge-collateral liquidity tail (2022 precedent: ~$1.9B margin call in 6 months), FERC rehearing/DC-Circuit reversal of the co-location order, nuclear outage concentration, AI-capex digestion. *Falsifier:* FERC order final + survives rehearing; no margin stress through a volatile season.

## 4. The 5 things that change the work
1. **GAAP NI is a trap** — Adj EBITDA was 6.3× GAAP NI in FY25; ~70% of Q1'26 GAAP NI was a non-cash hedge gain. *(FS)*
2. **Hedge-collateral liquidity is the real tail** — gross derivative liability peaked $11.6B (2.4× equity, Q2'22); a repeat 2× forward move could demand $2–4B margin vs ~$2.8B immediate dry powder. *(FS)*
3. **FERC co-location risk is LOW and declining for VST specifically** — the Dec-18-2025 order (193 FERC ¶61,217, EL25-49) blesses front-of-meter contract-demand co-location; VST's Meta deal is FTM and AWS sits in ERCOT (outside FERC RTO jurisdiction), so VST avoided Talen's rejected behind-the-meter structure. *(A5)*
4. **Nuclear PTC (45U) ≈ $545M / ~9% of FY25 EBITDA, sunsets 12/31/2032 and fades as power prices rise** — a floor, not growth; a quiet bear lever for the out-years. *(A4)*
5. **Positioning is a crowded long mid-unwind, not a squeeze** — SI +52% in shares since March (to 4.0% float) but DTC ~2.3 and cheap borrow; active funds exiting; insiders net sellers (all 10b5-1). *(A8)*

## 7. Preliminary Red Team view
Strongest S1/S2 fact the bull case underweights: **the open-generation book is ~42% of 2028 volume and rides ISO prices that, per EIA's own modeling, only spike in a high-demand scenario in ERCOT and barely move in PJM** — so 2028+ EBITDA growth above the contracted floor is a power-price bet, not a contracted annuity. Add the **45U PTC ~$545M sunsetting 2032** (a headwind that grows as the credit fades) and the **hedge-collateral tail**, and the "de-risked secular compounder" framing is partly a hedged-near-term / open-long-term structure wearing an annuity costume.

## 9. Preliminary direction
**Mild-bearish-to-neutral bias, source-conditional conviction.** Fundamentals and regulation are improving, but the question is whether ~9.6× 2026E EV/EBITDA / ~8.6% FCF yield adequately compensates for (a) a crowded long still unwinding, (b) open-book power-price sensitivity in the out-years, and (c) a real-but-tail hedge-collateral liquidity risk. This is a valuation/positioning call, not an earnings call. Phase 2 must pin down the defensible consensus variance and the peer-relative case before any rating.
