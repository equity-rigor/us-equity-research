## 5. The load-bearing risks
1. **Leverage at the top of target + no MLP interest tax shield (the load-bearing risk).** Net leverage ~4.4x sits at the **top** of the 4.0–4.5x target; LT debt $69,317M (S1: Q1'26 10-Q). As a pass-through, ET earns no entity-level interest tax shield, so rising rates hit distributable cash flow directly. The Street's re-rate is gated on a sub-4.0x print that may slip. *Mitigant:* ~1.7–1.8x DCF coverage, ~90% fee-based, deleveraging trajectory.
2. **Commodity/volume downcycle (the consensus context).** Though ~90% fee-based, volumes carry indirect commodity sensitivity; an oil sub-$55 / weak-NGL-spread downcycle caps Permian/NGL growth and trims FY27E EBITDA toward ~$17.5B (vs ~$19.7B base).
3. **K-1 / MLP structural discount.** UBTI for tax-exempt holders, state-filing friction, index-exclusion — a persistent discount to C-corp midstream (WMB/KMI). *This is the source of the value opportunity as much as a risk.*
4. **Acquisition treadmill.** Crestwood/WTG/Lotus/Sunoco lifted gross debt to ~$71B; integration is characterized as substantially complete, but acquisition ROIC and any large re-levering deal are watch items.

## 6. Consensus variance (the edge, honestly stated)
**One defensible, S1-evidenced load-bearing variance: a DOWNWARD edge on the 12-month MULTIPLE, not the estimates.** The Street's ~$23.5 PT (S4) implies a re-rate toward EPD-parity (~7.7x EV/EBITDA / ~8.2x P/DCF on FY27E); we hold the base at ~7.3x EV/EBITDA (7.5x P/DCF) because (i) net leverage is at the ~4.4x top of the 4.0–4.5x target (S1: Q1'26 10-Q $69,317M) and the re-rate is gated on a sub-4.0x print, and (ii) the K-1/UBTI structural discount versus C-corp midstream has persisted for years (sizing ~2.1pp; magnitude compounds a ~9% multiple gap and a ~9% more-conservative FY27–28 path). **Crucially, estimates are RISING** — FY26 Adjusted EBITDA guidance was raised ~$750M (+4%) at Q1'26 (S2) — so this is a multiple call, not an estimate call. It is **two-tailed** (backlog conversion is genuine upside; bull/strong_bull sit ABOVE the Street), which is why it supports **Buy below-Street, not Sell**. *Red-team note (G20):* an R-v2 base-rate attack (midstream names have re-rated 1.5–2.5 turns on deleveraging, e.g. WMB/OKE) **modified** rather than killed this — we lifted the base multiple to 7.5x and trimmed sizing to ~2.1pp, but the variance survives because those re-rates each followed a sub-4.0x print and (for WMB/KMI) a C-corp structure ET does not have.
