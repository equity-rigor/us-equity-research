## §1 Thesis in brief
FCX is the **large-cap US-listed leverage to the structural copper/electrification thesis** — ~3.4–3.6 Blb/yr of copper plus major gold (Grasberg gold credits) and molybdenum, a low-cost Americas portfolio, a fast-growing low-capital leaching "virtual mine," and downstream smelting. The view is **source-conditional** and the call is **fully valued at the high**. At $66.20 the probability-weighted return is **−2.5%** with a **left-skewed, fat-tailed** distribution. The bull leg is real: copper faces a structural deficit (declining grades, thin pipeline, grid/EV/AI demand), each $0.10/lb is ~$400M of EBITDA, Grasberg recovers in 2027, and leaching scales toward ~800 Mlb at <$1.00/lb. But the stock prices much of it: copper at ~$6.19/lb is ~40–55% above a $4.00–4.50 mid-cycle, FCX's ~10x EV/EBITDA is above its ~7.8x 5-yr average **on peak earnings** (double-counting the cycle), and ~40–50% of value rides on **Grasberg** — state-majority-controlled (MIND ID 51.24%), mid-recovery from a fatal mud-rush, on a high-risk block-cave ramp, and subject to Indonesian export/divestment policy (FCX's stake steps to ~37% from 2042). With consensus PTs only ~+4–6% above spot, the asymmetry is to the downside → **Hold, conditional on** copper holding and Grasberg recovering. The structural-deficit tail keeps it from a Sell. The actionable stance is **benchmark-weight/modest-underweight** and a re-entry on a copper dip below ~$5.50/lb or a Grasberg de-risk.

## §5 The load-bearing risks
1. **Copper price correction (the dominant lever).** ~40–55% above a $4.00–4.50 mid-cycle; Goldman sees a 2026 decline. With ~$400M EBITDA per $0.10/lb, a 15–20% correction removes ~$3.7–5.0B of a ~$13–14B EBITDA *and* miners de-rate the multiple at the same time → **~30–45% equity downside**. China property (~25% of world copper historically) remains impaired. *Mitigant:* structural deficit + declining grades support a higher floor than past cycles.
2. **Grasberg block-cave ramp + single-asset concentration.** The Sept-2025 mud-rush (7 fatalities, force majeure) cut the block-cave (~50% of PT-FI reserves); the phased restart (Blocks 2&3 Q2'26, 1S Q2'27) already slipped once and carries geotechnical tail risk. ~40–50% of value rides on this one asset. *Mitigant:* $699M insurance booked; DMLZ/Big Gossan restarted; full recovery targeted 2027.
3. **Indonesia resource nationalism.** MIND ID owns 51.24% of PT-FI; the Feb-2026 MoU extends the license past 2041 only via FCX divesting another ~12% (stake to ~37% from 2042); export permits are short-dated and tied to domestic smelting; FCX contests an export duty; the Manyar smelter restart is pending. *Mitigant:* license extended to 2041 + MoU; governance preserved through 2041.
4. **Premium-on-peak valuation.** ~10x EV/EBITDA vs ~7.8x 5-yr average on peak-cycle earnings, at the 52-week high with PTs only ~+4–6% above spot. *Mitigant:* below net-debt target; performance-based payout; SCCO trades far higher (relative cushion).
