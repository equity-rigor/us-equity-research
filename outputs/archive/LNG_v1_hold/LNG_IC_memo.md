## §1 Thesis in brief
Cheniere is the **blue-chip, fully-contracted leg of US LNG export** — the largest, lowest-cost, investment-grade platform (~52 mtpa operating today toward >60 mtpa, vs >100 mtpa permitted), running a **~90%-contracted take-or-pay book** with ~15-year weighted-average remaining SPA life and predominantly investment-grade counterparties (S1: LNG FY2025 10-K). It reached **investment grade at all three entities** (LNG Moody's Baa2, Feb'26; S&P BBB+; Fitch BBB), reloaded a **>$10B-through-2030 buyback**, and is compounding Distributable Cash Flow per share toward a ~$30 run-rate even on a flat platform. Yet at $239.40 the probability-weighted return is only **+4.9%**: the view is **source-conditional** and turns on **multiple durability, not estimates**. The bull anchors to the contracted annuity + capital-return compounding + brownfield FID optionality; the bear anchors to a **premium multiple carried into the biggest LNG supply wave in history**, where the variable upside the stock re-rated on is contractually tiny (open book <1 mtpa in 2026; <$50M EBITDA per $1/MMBtu) and the marginal book likely recontracts at lower fixed fees. The risk is genuinely two-sided → **Hold**, **conditional on** the FY2026 raise proving durable. The actionable edge is **relative value (long VG / short LNG)** and a sub-$200 / new-FID re-entry, not a directional long at $239.

## §5 The load-bearing risks
1. **Global LNG oversupply / spread compression (2026–2028).** ~193 mtpa of new liquefaction starts 2024–2028 (Qatar NFE/NFS +48, Plaquemines ~20, Golden Pass ~16–18, Port Arthur ~13, CP2 ~28; S3), ~57 mtpa in 2026 alone, into decelerating demand → JKM toward $7–8/MMBtu, HH-to-delivered spread toward shipping cost. *Mitigant:* ~90% contracted; the open slice is <1 mtpa in 2026 (<$50M EBITDA per $1/MMBtu).
2. **Recontracting / marginal-fee compression (the consensus variance).** New SPA/FID fees are undisclosed; Wolfe assumes recontracting toward ~$2.00/MMBtu vs a legacy book cited ~$3/MMBtu+ (S3). Train 7 / Corpus Christi 4 (FID early-2027) and SPL Expansion FID land **into the trough**, risking marginal fees below legacy and capping DCF/share growth.
3. **Valuation de-rate / rate sensitivity.** A **premium ~10.2x FY26E EV/EBITDA** (vs an 8–11x midstream band), <1% dividend yield, long-duration cash-flow stream. Base rates: premium energy-infra multiples compressed 2+ turns through the 2014–15 and 2020 oversupply cycles **even when EBITDA held**. A re-rate to ~8.5–9x is ~3 turns / ~$130/share of EV; the thin yield offers little carry on the way down.
4. **Crowded-long unwind.** ~96% Street Buy, +27% median PT ($304.50), 15 Strong Buys, stock ~20% off its $300.89 52-week high; net insider selling YTD. *Mitigant:* low short interest (~1.5% float, DTC ~1.8) = no forced unwind; the >$10B buyback + passive flows absorb sellers; estimates rising not falling.
