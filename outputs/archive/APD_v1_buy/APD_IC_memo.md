## §5 The load-bearing risks
1. **Residual hydrogen impairment tail.** NEOM (~$8.5B JV, APD ~1/3, >90% complete) and Louisiana blue-H2 (~$7B, Yara FID targeted mid-2026, **not signed**) carry the largest residual asset values. A slipped NEOM offtake or failed Yara FID → a further **>$1B impairment** (after ~$3.7B already, S2). *Mitigant:* NEOM ~90% sunk; Yara 25-yr offtake in advanced negotiation.
2. **FCF inflection fails / debt-funded dividend.** Interest expense +23% YoY (S1: Q2 FY26 10-Q); both agencies negative outlook. If capex re-accelerates or OCF disappoints, FCF stays negative and the coverage question re-opens. *Mitigant:* capex guided ~$4.0B FY26 (down ~$3B); TTM FCF already −$1.25B vs −$3.8B.
3. **Valuation / rate sensitivity.** A low-beta dividend-aristocrat with ~$15.8B net debt — doubly rate-sensitive. A higher-for-longer path or failed re-rate could widen the Linde discount.
4. **Linde out-executes / discount stays wide.** If Linde keeps widening its margin/ROIC lead (~39% EBITDA margin), the ~7-8-turn discount could be permanent. *Mitigant:* even partial convergence to ~24.5x drives the base case; activist board enforcing discipline.
