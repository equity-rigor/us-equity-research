## 2. What Vistra Is

Vistra is the **largest US competitive (non-regulated, merchant) integrated power company** — a ~50+ GW diversified generation fleet (natural gas, nuclear, coal, solar, storage) vertically integrated with a leading **competitive retail electricity** business (~5M customers; TXU Energy, Dynegy, Ambit, et al.). It operates in deregulated markets: **ERCOT** (Texas), **PJM** (East), and ISO-NE/NYISO/CAISO (West). Reporting segments: Retail, Texas, East, West, Sunset/Asset Closure, Corporate.

The structural differentiator versus pure-play nuclear peer **Constellation (CEG)** is the **integrated hedge**: the retail book (management cited ~100% 2026 coverage) naturally offsets generation, smoothing merchant price volatility. (S1-1, S5-2)

**Fleet de-risking via long-dated, investment-grade PPAs is the core 2024→2026 story:**
- **AWS** — 20-year PPA (+ up to 20yr extension options) for **1,200 MW** carbon-free from **Comanche Peak** nuclear (TX); deliveries begin **Q4 2027**, full by **2032**; management guides **~8–10% incremental Adjusted FCFbG** accretion at full utilization. (S5-1)
- **Meta** — 20-year PPAs for **>2,600 MW** of energy/capacity/uprates across the **PJM nuclear fleet** (Beaver Valley PA; Perry & Davis-Besse OH). (S5 cluster)

**Fleet scaled by acquisition:** Energy Harbor (~4 GW nuclear, ~$3.4B, 2024) → **Lotus** (2.6 GW gas, closed Nov 2025) → **Cogentrix** (5,496 MW gas, **$4B**, announced Jan 5 2026, **expected close mid-to-late 2026**). (S5-2)

## 4. Forensic Accounting Read

**Headline: high earnings quality on the usual failure dimensions; the real wedge is hedge mark-to-market, not SBC.**

- **SBC immaterial** (~0.6% of revenue, $113M FY25) — this is a capital-intensive generator, not a tech compounder. SBC-in-FCF distortion is negligible.
- **The non-GAAP / GAAP gap is dominated by ASC 815 unrealized hedge mark-to-market**, not "one-time" restructuring. GAAP NI swung **$1.49B → $2.66B → $0.94B** (FY23→25) while Ongoing Adj EBITDA rose steadily ($5.64B → $5.91B). FY25 GAAP NI was depressed by an **~$808M unrealized hedge loss**; Q1 2026 GAAP NI was *boosted* by an **~$723M unrealized hedge gain**. **Implication: anchor on Adj EBITDA / FCFbG, not GAAP EPS.** Phase 1 must split realized vs unrealized and assess hedge-book **margin/collateral liquidity** (2022-style spike risk).
- **Goodwill modest** — $2.81B, ~6.8% of assets; rose only ~$227M across three acquisitions (purchase price booked mostly to PP&E/intangibles, consistent with asset-heavy deals).
- **Auditor: Deloitte & Touche LLP, continuous since 2002** (24 yrs), ratified at the April 29 2026 AGM. **No restatement (8-K 4.02), no going-concern.** (S2-1)
- **Leverage:** net debt ~$19.3B ≈ ~3× 2026E EBITDA midpoint; **IG-rated at two agencies** as of Q1 2026 — partially neutralizes the balance-sheet bear point.

## 6. Regulatory Desk

Export-control / sanctions desk is **N/A** (US-domiciled domestic utility — no BIS Entity List / OFAC / CFIUS exposure). Live matters are sector-specific:

1. **FERC — large-load co-location interconnection** (the **Talen/AWS Susquehanna ISA** precedent). This is the single most load-bearing regulatory question: it governs whether behind-the-meter datacenter PPAs at nuclear plants clear on the economics Vistra has signed. Two-sided catalyst for 2026.
2. **NRC** — operating licenses/uprates for Comanche Peak, Beaver Valley, Davis-Besse, Perry (Meta deal references Ohio uprates). Routine.
3. **EPA** — GHG/MATS/effluent rules driving ~**4.6 GW coal retirements 2027–2028** (closure/ARO drag, partly offset by tightening capacity).
4. **PJM / ERCOT capacity design** — PJM Base Residual Auctions cleared at **FERC offer caps ($329–333/MW-day, 2026/27 & 2027/28)**, ~**40–63% driven by data-center load**. Durability of capacity revenue (and price-cap/market-power mitigation) is a key swing factor. (S5-3)

## 8. Three Competing Hypotheses (to test in Phases 1–3 — not adjudicated here)

1. **H1 — AI-power super-cycle (bull):** Scarcity of 24/7 carbon-free + dispatchable baseload, long-dated IG PPAs (AWS, Meta), and capacity prices at caps de-risk merchant cash flow and justify re-rating toward quasi-regulated multiples; ~8–9% FCF yield funds a share-count-shrinking buyback.
2. **H2 — commodity quality-trap (bear):** Merchant power is cyclical commodity exposure narrated as secular growth; Adj EBITDA flatters noisy GAAP earnings; price/weather/capacity mean-reversion plus ~$20B acquisition-led debt and coal-retirement drag mean ~9.6× already prices the story.
3. **H3 — regulatory & concentration tail:** FERC co-location ruling, nuclear outage concentration, hyperscaler counterparty concentration, and an AI-capex slowdown are the downside tails.
