## 1. Candidate variance tests (keep only defensible)

### CANDIDATE 1 — Retail normalization (FY25 $1.622B → ~$1.4B medium-term, −14%)

**Test:** Does Street model the step-down, or extrapolate peak retail?

**Evidence:**
- Mgmt: FY25 Retail Adj EBITDA = **$1.622B record**, explicitly driven by **non-recurring tailwinds (supply-cost benefits + Energy Harbor gains) "not expected to repeat"**; guided DOWN to **~$1.4B medium-term**; Q1'26 call reiterated retail "to moderate from the record" (**S1**, Q4'25/Q1'26 transcripts; corroborated by Yahoo/SEC 8-K).
- The step-down is **mgmt-guided and on the record** — i.e., it is S1/S3 disclosed, not a secret. Sophisticated sell-side (the 13–19 analysts) generally builds segment models off company guidance.
- BUT: total-company FY26 EBITDA consensus is tracking the **TOP** of the $6.8–7.6B guide (~$7.35–7.4B; **S4**) while mgmt's own midpoint is $7.2B. A −$0.2B retail headwind is consistent with a $7.2B midpoint but NOT with a $7.4B consensus unless offset by generation/capacity upside.

**Classification:** This is **disclosed, not a variance.** The −14% retail normalization is company guidance (S1/S3); the Street can read the transcript. There is no informational edge in "retail will normalize" — mgmt said so. The only residual edge is *if* consensus's top-of-guide $7.4B EBITDA implicitly assumes retail HOLDS near peak AND generation rises — i.e., the retail step-down is a constraint on how high the rest of the book can carry consensus. That is a weak, second-order point.

**VERDICT: DROP as a standalone variance.** Magnitude ~−$0.2B (−14% of segment, ~−3% of total EBITDA) is real but fully disclosed (S1) → no edge vs an informed Street. Retains value only as supporting color that top-of-guide consensus is optimistic. *Probability Street is "wrong" on retail: <25% — they have the transcript.*

### CANDIDATE 2 — Open-book 2028 power-price sensitivity (~42% open) ★ STRONGEST

**Test:** Is Street's 2027–2028 EBITDA assuming sustained peak power/capacity prices on the open book?

**Evidence:**
- Hedge ratios (mgmt, Feb'26): **98% / 84% / 58% hedged for 2026 / 2027 / 2028** → **~42% of 2028 generation is OPEN** and rides spot/forward ISO power (**S1**, Q3'25 + Q1'26 disclosures; Luminix S5 corroborates).
- EIA modeling (primary, government source): under a **high data-center-demand scenario**, **2027 PJM wholesale prices rise only ~+4% (+$2.60/MWh)** vs baseline — because PJM "is interconnected … and has access to more coal and natural gas capacity." ERCOT 2027 rises **+79% (+$37/MWh) but ONLY in the high-demand scenario** and concentrated in late-summer low-wind peaks; ERCOT is isolated so price response is "more acute" but scenario-conditional (**S1-equivalent S-gov**, EIA Today in Energy id=67344). *EIA has NO 2028 price detail published — the out-year the Street is extrapolating is literally beyond the public modeling horizon.*
- Street's own bull spine = **14.7% rev CAGR to 2028 + revenue carried to $26.8B** (**S4**), and bull PTs explicitly cite "unhedged volumes rolling into an increasingly tight market" (**S5**). I.e., **consensus is monetizing the 42%-open 2028 book at sustained-elevated power AND continued tightening** — a power-price BET dressed as an annuity.

**The variance:** Street treats 2027–2028 open-book EBITDA growth as quasi-contracted; in reality only ~9% of fleet MW (the AWS+Meta PPAs, once ramped) is contracted, and the EIA's own modeling says the *non-ERCOT* upside is small (~+4% PJM) and the *ERCOT* upside is high-demand-scenario-only and seasonally narrow. If 2028 power normalizes (mid-cycle, not peak), the open 42% delivers materially less than the $7.6B+ 2027 / continued-growth-2028 consensus implies.

**Classification: SCENARIO-WEIGHT variance (with estimate-level 2028 tail).** Not a near-term estimate cut (2026 is 98% hedged — locked). The disagreement is on the **probability distribution of 2028**, where the Street effectively assigns near-base-case weight to a high-demand/peak-price outcome.

- **Direction:** DOWNWARD (vs consensus 2028 / terminal).
- **Magnitude:** 42% open book × VST ~145–150 TWh generation ≈ ~60 TWh open in 2028. A normalization of ~$10–15/MWh off peak on the open slice ≈ **~$0.6–0.9B EBITDA, i.e., ~8–12% below a peak-extrapolated 2028** (modeled from EIA price deltas + open-MWh; price input S5-modeled).
- **My probability of being right (that 2028 open-book is over-modeled by Street):** **~60%.** EIA primary modeling is on my side for PJM; the risk to my view is that ERCOT high-demand DOES materialize (data-center load is accelerating, per Phase 2 A3) and a 2028 ERCOT spike could *exceed* my normalization. So this is a genuine two-tail, not a slam-dunk short.
- **Scenario-weight sizing impact:** shifts ~**8–12 pp** of probability mass from base/bull into bear/strong-bear for the 2028+ leg; in a 5-scenario frame this is the single biggest lever on the terminal-value / out-year EBITDA and supports trimming any bull weight that rests on "tight market forever."
- **Evidence_ref:** EIA Today-in-Energy id=67344 (gov primary, +4% PJM / +79% ERCOT-high-demand-only); VST hedge ratios 98/84/58% (**S1**). **NOT S4/S5-only.** ✔

**VERDICT: KEEP — this is the load-bearing defensible variance.**

### CANDIDATE 3 — PTC sunset (45U, ~$545M, end-2031/2032)

**Test:** Does Street terminal value account for the fade?

**Evidence:**
- 45U Zero-Emission Nuclear PTC = up to $15/MWh (0.3¢/kWh base, inflation-adjusted), **expires end-2031/2032** (final OBBBA set a hard cutoff ~end-2031; earlier phase-out draft was dropped) (**S1-gov**, Congress CRS IN12557 / Cornell LII 26 USC 45U).
- **CRITICAL mechanic:** 45U is a **contract-for-differences that PHASES OUT at HIGHER power prices** — the credit is reduced as the nuclear facility's gross receipts rise above the threshold (**S1-gov**, CRS / Crux / law.cornell). VST's own framing (Phase 1 A4): ~$545M ≈ ~9% of FY25 EBITDA, "a floor, not growth," that **fades as power prices rise** (**S1**, Phase 1 A4 from transcripts/10-K).

**The key analytical point (anti-double-count):** the PTC and the open-book power-price upside are **INVERSELY correlated by statute.** The Street CANNOT simultaneously model (a) sustained peak power prices on the open book (Candidate 2) AND (b) a full ~$545M PTC — high power prices mechanically shrink the PTC. So either power normalizes (Candidate-2 downside bites, PTC floor partly offsets) or power spikes (PTC fades). This caps the upside symmetry the bull case claims.

**Classification:** **estimate-level (long-dated) variance**, DOWNWARD, but **largely self-hedging vs Candidate 2** and very long-dated (2031+).
- **Direction:** DOWNWARD to terminal value only.
- **Magnitude:** ~$545M (~9% of EBITDA) steps off after 2031 in a high-power world (where it has already faded) or in any world by 2032 — but in DCF terms a 2032 step-down 6 years out is ~5–7% of NPV at most, and partly offset by the power prices that caused it.
- **Probability of being right Street under-models this:** **~45%** — disciplined DCFs DO taper terminal credits; many do not. Low conviction.
- **Sizing impact:** ~**2–3 pp** terminal-leg haircut; mostly subsumed by Candidate 2.
- **Evidence_ref:** CRS IN12557 / 26 USC 45U (gov primary). ✔

**VERDICT: KEEP as a SUPPORTING/anti-double-count point, not a standalone load-bearing variance.** Its real value is forcing the memo to NOT credit both peak-power AND full-PTC (a common bull error).

### CANDIDATE 4 — PPA conversion optionality (~3.2 GW nuclear + gas co-loc; Comanche Unit 2 ~1,200 MW uncontracted)

**Test:** If Street gives ZERO credit to un-signed MW, a conversion is an UPward variance. But is it real edge or hope?

**Evidence (from Phase 2 A3, primary-verified):**
- ~**3.2 GW uncontracted nuclear** (Beaver Valley remainder + Comanche Peak Unit 2 ~1,200 MW + ~200 MW uprates) is mgmt-quantified as a "near-term contracting priority" (**S1**, Q1'26 transcript). Gas co-location pipeline real but unquantified (≥2 named-anonymous counterparties).
- Mgmt $-quantified next leg: Meta+Cogentrix economics "$700–750M annual opportunity" (**S1**, Q4'25).
- **DECISIVE NEGATIVE:** Phase 2 A3 found **ZERO new signed MW between Meta (Jan'26) and the Q1'26 call (May'26)** — mgmt repeatedly declined LOI/MOU counts. The next leg is **conversations, not paper** (**S1**, A3 verdict).

**Classification:** **catalyst-timing variance**, UPWARD — but **un-triggered**.
- **Direction:** UPWARD *if* a deal signs.
- **Magnitude:** a Comanche Unit 2 (~1,200 MW) signing ≈ another ~$0.3B+ FCFbG/PPA leg (~AWS-equivalent), but it is OPTION not BACKLOG.
- **Probability of being right (that an UPward variance materializes near-term):** **~30–35%** that ≥1 material MW signs in the next 2–3 quarters; LOWER that Street gives "zero" credit — the consensus already carries 14.7% rev CAGR and a 17x exit multiple, which arguably ALREADY prices the optionality. So even if a deal signs, the *variance vs an already-optimistic Street* is small.
- **Sizing impact:** ~**3–5 pp** to bull weight ONLY upon a signed catalyst; today = ~0 pp because there is no trigger and Street isn't at zero credit.
- **Evidence_ref:** Q1'26 transcript / 8-K (**S1**).

**VERDICT: DROP as a current load-bearing variance.** It is a real OPTION but (a) un-triggered (zero new MW in 5 months) and (b) Street's growth-extrapolated PT plausibly already embeds it — so it is not a *variance vs Street*, it's the bull's existing case. Keep only as a tracked catalyst (would flip to UPward variance ON a signing).

### CANDIDATE 5 — Capacity-price durability (PJM cleared at FERC cap 2 auctions)

**Test:** Does Street extrapolate cap-level capacity revenue?

**Evidence:**
- PJM cleared at the **FERC cap two straight auctions**: **2026/27 BRA at $329.17/MW-day**, **2027/28 BRA at $333.44/MW-day** (**S1-equivalent S-gov**, PJM Inside Lines / power-eng). Bull PTs explicitly cite "record PJM capacity prices" (**S5**).
- **DECISIVE STRUCTURAL FACT:** the **$325 cap / $175 floor "collar" is a temporary FERC settlement** (Shapiro complaint origin) that covers ONLY **2026/27, 2027/28, and (newly extended) 2028/29 + 2029/30** delivery years — and **EXPIRES after 2029/30** (**S1-gov**, PJM Inside Lines / Diversegy / Utility Dive). The **2028/29 BRA closes July 7 2026** at a cap **lowered from $550 to ~$325/MW-day** (**S1-gov**, PJM/Utility Dive).

**The variance:** Two competing reads, and the timing matters:
1. The cap is a CEILING — clearing AT it 2 years running means the *equilibrium* price wants to be ABOVE the cap, so when the collar expires after 2029/30, capacity could re-rate HIGHER (bullish).
2. BUT the collar is a political artifact (Shapiro/affordability pressure); the same political force that capped it could re-impose limits, and the 2028/29 cap was just CUT from $550 to $325. Street extrapolating cap-level (~$329–333) revenue as a durable annuity into the open 2028 book is extrapolating a **politically-administered price, not a market-cleared one** — fragile in either direction.

**Classification:** **scenario-weight variance, AMBIGUOUS direction** (cap could be a floor-on-scarcity OR a politically-fragile ceiling). Because direction is genuinely two-sided, it is NOT a clean load-bearing variance — it widens the 2028 distribution rather than shifting the mean.
- **Magnitude:** PJM capacity is a meaningful but not dominant slice of VST EBITDA; a ±$100/MW-day swing on VST's PJM UCAP is material to the open 2028 book but bounded.
- **Probability:** ~50/50 on direction → low decision value as a directional variance.
- **Sizing impact:** ~**3–4 pp** added to BOTH tails (variance-widening), reinforcing Candidate 2's "wider 2028 distribution" conclusion.
- **Evidence_ref:** PJM BRA results + FERC collar dockets (**S1-gov**). ✔

**VERDICT: KEEP as a variance-WIDENER supporting Candidate 2, NOT a standalone directional edge.** It strengthens the "2028 is a distribution, not a point" thesis but does not by itself support a directional non-Hold rating.

### The honest answer

**There is ONE defensible, evidenced, load-bearing variance: Candidate 2 — the Street over-models the 42%-open 2028 book at sustained peak power/capacity prices.** It is the only candidate that is (a) NOT already disclosed/priced (unlike retail), (b) directionally clear (unlike capacity), (c) triggered/real today (unlike PPA optionality), and (d) backed by **primary government (EIA) modeling** that says the non-ERCOT upside is small (~+4% PJM) and the ERCOT upside is high-demand-scenario-only — i.e., S1-grade, not S4/S5 hand-waving. Candidates 3 and 5 SUPPORT it (PTC fades into the same peak-power world; capacity cap is politically fragile and expires after 2029/30), and the anti-double-count point (PTC ↓ as power ↑) caps the bull's claimed symmetry.

**BUT — three honest caveats that prevent this from supporting a high-conviction directional (Sell/Underweight) rating:**
1. **2026 is 98% hedged and 2027 is 84% hedged** (**S1**) — the variance is almost entirely a **2028+/terminal** disagreement. Near-term estimates are locked; I cannot claim a 2026/2027 estimate cut. The Street's 2026/2027 numbers are essentially un-falsifiable-down.
2. **My probability of being right is ~60%, not 80%+** — ERCOT high-demand (which Phase 2 A3 shows is ACCELERATING) could make 2028 ERCOT spike and *invalidate* the normalization. This is a two-tailed distribution, not a one-way short.
3. **The multiple has ALREADY compressed to ~9.8x** (vs CEG ~11.7x) and PTs were cut on multiple, not estimates (**S4**) — so much of the "2028 isn't a peak annuity" skepticism may already be in the ~$142 price (which sits ~37% below the $227 median PT). The market may have already discounted the variance even though consensus *estimates* haven't moved.

### RATING IMPLICATION

**This is a SCENARIO-WEIGHT / DISTRIBUTION variance that supports a NEUTRAL-to-mild-bearish stance and a wider-than-consensus 2028 outcome band — it does NOT support a high-conviction non-Hold long, and it is too 2-tailed (and possibly already-priced at 9.8x / $142) to support a high-conviction short.**

→ **The memo should be labeled primarily "consensus-anchored" with a Hold ceiling on the LONG side**, BUT with one defensible, evidenced load-bearing variance (Candidate 2, downward, scenario-weight, ~60% conviction, 8–12 pp sizing) that justifies **underweighting the bull-case 2028 extrapolation relative to the Street** and refusing to credit the "tight-market-forever" annuity framing. The variance is real and S1-evidenced enough to AVOID a consensus-Buy and to size BELOW the crowd, but not strong/near-dated enough to flip to an outright Sell. **Verdict: defensible DOWNWARD scenario-weight edge on the out-years exists → caps the rating at Hold/Neutral with a below-consensus tilt; NOT a clean directional non-Hold.**

If a near-term catalyst fires (Candidate 4: a new signed PPA on Comanche Unit 2 or gas co-location), the balance flips toward an UPward catalyst-timing variance and the rating could move to a tactical long — but as of 2026-06-09, zero new MW have signed since Jan'26, so that trigger is absent.
