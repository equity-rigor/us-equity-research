## 1) BASE-RATE ANALYSIS ‚Äî Do merchant-IPP re-ratings persist 24 months after a capacity-price peak?

**Finding: No. Merchant-power multiple expansions on top of cycle-peak commodity/capacity prices have a poor 24-month persistence record, and the current setup double-counts peak multiple √ó peak EBITDA.**

**The double-counting problem (core bear point):**
- **PJM capacity is at the administrative ceiling, not a free-market peak.** 2026/27 BRA cleared **$329.17/MW-day** at the FERC cap; 2027/28 BRA (Dec-2025) cleared **$333.44/MW-day**, again *at the cap*, +1.3% (S1, PJM). When offers clear "at the cap," upside is **truncated by regulation** while downside runs to the **$179.55/MW-UCAP floor** ‚Äî a potential **‚àí46%** move in the capacity revenue line with no symmetric upside (S1, PJM 2027/28 BRA report).
- **The private market disagrees with the public multiple.** VST bought **Cogentrix at ~7x 2026 EBITDA** ($4.0B net, 5,496 MW, Jan-2026); **NRG bought 13 GW of gas at 7.5x** (S2, Utility Dive / Power Magazine / company). Sophisticated strategic buyers ‚Äî including VST's own management ‚Äî value these exact assets at **7‚Äì7.5x**. VST's public stock at **~9.4x** embeds a **~25‚Äì30% narrative premium** over where the assets actually transact.

**Base-rate conclusion:** A ~9.4‚Äì10x EV/EBITDA stamped on EBITDA that is (a) ~100% hedged in 2026 at cycle-high forwards and (b) underpinned by cap-pinned capacity revenue is a **peak-multiple-on-peak-earnings** configuration. History (NRG '15, VST's own 2018‚Äì20 range) says the multiple reverts toward **7‚Äì8x** within 24 months of a power/capacity peak in the majority of merchant-power cycles.

## 3) PTC CLIFF ‚Äî quantifying the ¬ß45U headwind into terminal value

- **Cliff pulled forward one year.** OBBBA (P.L. 119-21, signed **7/4/2025**) sets a **hard ¬ß45U termination at end-2031** (not the IRA's 2032). Full value through 2031, then **zero in 2032** (S1/S2, statute / Steptoe / BPC).
- **Phase-down before the cliff.** ¬ß45U credit = 0.3c/kWh baseline, **reduced 16% of the excess of gross receipts over ~2.5c/kWh, reaching ZERO at ~4.375c/kWh** (~$43.75/MWh). Texas nuclear (Comanche Peak) realizing power **above ~$44/MWh erases the PTC entirely ‚Äî well before 2031** (S1/S2, IRS / CRS / Crux). The bull's own "high power prices" thesis is **self-cannibalizing**: higher ERCOT prices *eliminate* the ~$545M PTC.
- **TV impact:** ~$545M ‚âà **~9% of EBITDA** and is **~95%+ margin / near-100% FCF**, so it is disproportionately valuable in a DCF terminal. At ~9.4x it represents **~$5.1B of EV (~7% of EV / ~$15/share)** that **structurally vanishes by 2032** and partially fades earlier if prices rise. A terminal multiple that ignores this is overstated.
