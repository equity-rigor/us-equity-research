## 2. Cycle Position

### CYCLE CALL: **MID-CYCLE, transitioning toward late-mid in the AI-power upcycle.** Merchant power is in a structural **mid-cycle uptrend** on tightening supply/demand, but the *easy* re-rating (capacity prices, nuclear PPA premia) is largely behind us. We are **not early** (the demand thesis is consensus and partly priced) and **not yet late-peak** (physical supply tightness persists through 2027–28 and forward curves still ascend). This favors H1 (supercycle re-rating, mostly recognized) with rising weight on H2 (quality-trap/normalization risk emerging at the margin).

**Anchoring indicators:**

**Why mid, not early:** PJM cleared at the **FERC-imposed cap two consecutive auctions** — the cap exists precisely because prices would otherwise blow out, signaling the supply scarcity is *already* recognized and politically contested (ratepayer backlash). Nuclear baseload is commanding premium PPAs and hyperscalers procured ~6 GW in 2025 plus Meta's 6.6 GW in Jan 2026. These are late-ish signs of a tight market, not the first innings.

**Why not late-peak:** Physical bottlenecks (gas turbines sold out to 2028–30, interconnection queues) prevent a near-term supply flood; ERCOT reserve margin still tightens into 2027; Henry Hub forward curve rises into 2027 (supportive of spark spreads + power prices). The supply response is *delayed*, extending the up-cycle.

## 3. Supply / Demand (next 24 months by ISO)

### Additions vs retirements
- **PJM:** Cleared 2027/28 auction **fell 5.2% short of the reliability requirement — first shortfall in PJM history (S1, PJM Dec 2025)**. Coal: ~34 GW retired since 2013, **another ~13.25 GW retiring 2024–28**; 24–58 GW (up to 30% of installed) at retirement risk by 2030 **(S5, Concentric/PJM-derived)**. New build skews gas + storage but lags.
- **ERCOT:** Reserve margin falls **17.2% (2026) → 9.0% (2027)** as load outruns adds (S1, ERCOT CDR May 2025). Peak demand could rise from ~98 GW (2026) to >111 GW (2032).
- **ISO-NE / NYISO / CAISO:** Comparatively balanced near-term; ISO-NE FCA prices flat (~$2.59/kW-mo). ISO-NE moving to a "prompt" (month-ahead) capacity auction by ~June 2028.

### Data-center pipeline (announced GW — note: heavily over-counted)
- **PJM:** 2025 Long-Term Load Forecast = **+32 GW peak load 2024–30, data centers = 94%** of it **(S1, PJM 2025 LTLF)**.
- **ERCOT:** **>233 GW** of large-load interconnection requests, **>70% data centers (S5, Utility Dive / ERCOT 2026)** — vastly exceeds plausible builds; signal-to-noise low.
- PJM Cycle 1 queue: **811 projects / 220 GW**, led by gas (105.8 GW) + storage (67.5 GW) (S5, PowerMag 2026). Gas now **106 GW = the largest** category in PJM's reopened queue (S5, Utility Dive 2026).

### Gas-turbine supply constraint (THE physical bottleneck)
- Three OEMs (GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi Power) supply **>75%** of large turbines; all booked to **late 2028 at the earliest** **(S5, Bloomberg/RMI/primary.vc, 2025–26)**.
- **GE Vernova:** targeting ≥110 GW backlog + slot reservations by YE2026; orders being discussed out to 2030.
- **Mitsubishi Power:** sold out into 2028; today's orders arrive 2028–2030.
- **Siemens Energy:** record ~€131B order backlog.
- **Implication:** New dispatchable gas supply cannot meaningfully relieve PJM/ERCOT before ~2028–29. This *protects* incumbent merchant economics (VST, CEG) through the bottleneck window — a core H1 support and a direct rebuttal to near-term overbuild fears.

### Transmission / interconnection + behind-the-meter debate
- PJM processed ~170 GW since 2023; ~63 GW transition queue remained mid-2025; interconnection + transmission remain gating constraints.
- **Behind-the-meter (BTM) / co-location** is the key structural debate. Hyperscalers want to plug directly into plants (bypassing grid charges); IPPs and FERC are negotiating the rules (Section 4). Outcome shapes whether IPP nuclear/gas serves load **front-of-the-meter (grid-priced, capacity-eligible)** or **behind-the-meter (bilateral, potentially bypassing IPP grid revenue stack)**.

## 5. Bull vs Bear (industry-level)

### BULL CASE (H1 supercycle)
1. **Demand is structural and physically supply-constrained.** EIA's strongest 4-yr demand growth since 2000; PJM's 2027/28 auction **fell 5.2% short of reliability needs — first shortfall ever** (S1). Scarcity is realized, not hypothetical.
2. **Gas-turbine sold-out window (to 2028–30)** caps the supply response, protecting incumbent merchant spark spreads and capacity revenue through the bottleneck.
3. **Capacity prices at FERC caps two auctions running** ($329 → $333/MW-day); the cap is the only thing holding prices down — bullish for the contracted/uncontracted revenue stack.
4. **Nuclear baseload commands premium long-dated PPAs** (~6 GW procured 2025; Meta 6.6 GW Jan 2026), de-risking IPP cash flows and re-rating multiples toward contracted-utility levels.
5. **Henry Hub rising into 2027** (+33%) lifts the marginal cost of generation → higher power prices for low-cost nuclear/efficient gas incumbents.
- **Falsifier:** Two consecutive ISO auctions (PJM BRA, ERCOT reserve-margin updates) clearing *materially below* prior prints, OR an EIA/PJM load-forecast cut for data centers — would break the scarcity thesis.

### BEAR CASE (steelmanned — H2 quality trap / H3 regulatory tail)
1. **Power-price normalization as supply catches up.** EIA itself models the 2027 ERCOT spike as a *high-demand scenario*; base case is only ~$47/MWh. **PJM under high-demand sees only ~+4%** (vs ERCOT +79%) because it has coal/gas slack and eastern interconnection (S1, EIA Mar 2026). The marginal-price upside is regionally narrow and scenario-dependent — **strongest single bear fact.**
2. **AI-capex digestion / overbuild.** Hyperscaler 2026 capex $635–690B (+67–74% y/y) is outrunning cloud revenue; Amazon FCF projected negative 2026; >$400B hyperscaler debt issuance expected (S5, Morgan Stanley/Allianz 2026). Announced DC pipelines (ERCOT 233 GW; PJM queue 220 GW) are **3–5x** any plausible build — a demand air-pocket would leave merchant capacity long.
3. **Behind-the-meter / co-location bypass risk to IPPs.** If FERC's PJM ruling and ERCOT SB6 ultimately enable hyperscalers to **self-supply BTM**, IPPs could be disintermediated from the grid revenue stack (capacity + ancillary), turning a sellers' market into bilateral price-taking.
4. **Regulatory/political ratepayer backlash.** PJM capacity costs added ~$9.3B to ratepayer bills; data centers blamed for 63% of the increase. Governors/legislators are intervening; FERC caps and cost-allocation reforms can compress the very prices the bull case relies on.
5. **Nuclear-concentration tail (H3).** A single forced outage at a contracted nuclear unit (VST Comanche Peak; CEG/Talen fleets) imperils high-margin PPA delivery — idiosyncratic but high-severity.
- **Falsifier:** A hyperscaler capex *cut* or DC-project cancellation wave, OR a FERC final order that explicitly permits unrestricted BTM co-location at scale — would confirm bear.

## 6. Confidence / Gaps

- **Freshest (high confidence):** PJM 2027/28 auction price + shortfall (Dec 2025, S1); Constellation-Calpine close (Jan 2026, S1/S5); FERC co-location order + compliance timeline (Dec 2025–Feb 2026, S1); EIA STEO Henry Hub & demand (Jan/May 2026, S1); ERCOT SB6 PUCT draft rule (Mar 2026, S5).
- **Stalest / to-refresh:** ERCOT CDR reserve margins (May 2025 — a 2026 CDR likely supersedes); ISO-NE FCA figures (FCA16/17 vintage); IEA 9%-by-2030 share (2025 model).
- **Could not fully verify:** Exact pro-forma VST fleet GW post-Cogentrix (range ~50–56 GW across sources); precise nuclear PPA $/MWh for the AWS-Comanche Peak deal (not publicly disclosed; SMR $60–80 used as proxy — caveat: SMR ≠ existing-fleet pricing); CAISO/NYISO near-term specifics (light coverage).
- **Where respected sources disagree:** **(a)** AI demand durability — IEA/EIA/BNEF see structural growth; Morgan Stanley/Allianz flag capex-vs-revenue gap and overbuild. **(b)** Price upside breadth — EIA shows ERCOT highly leveraged (+79% scenario) but PJM muted (+4%), so the "merchant" thesis is **not uniform across ISOs**. **(c)** BTM outcome — legal commentators split on whether FERC's framework helps or constrains IPP grid revenue.

**Net A1 view:** Mid-cycle, supply-constrained, structurally bullish through the 2027–28 turbine/interconnection bottleneck (H1), with the principal risk being **regional/scenario-dependent price normalization + BTM bypass** (H2/H3) rather than an imminent demand collapse.
