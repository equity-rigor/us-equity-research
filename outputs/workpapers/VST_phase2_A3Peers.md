**S-tags:** VST fleet/nuclear/retail (S5, useluminix/PRNewswire 2026; S1 VST 8-Ks). CEG 32.4 GW nuclear + ~55 GW total + $17.5B debt (S5 FinancialContent Mar 2026; S1 CEG Q1'26 May 2026). **Contracting / hedge differentiator (the crux of VST's identity):**
- **VST** is the only peer with a *structural integrated retail hedge*: ~5M retail customers provide a natural short against the generation length, dampening merchant-price volatility and monetizing the retail margin. This is VST's durable differentiator vs CEG (wholesale/PPA-led) and TLN (pure merchant).
- **CEG** contracts via long-dated hyperscaler PPAs (Microsoft/TMI-Crane 835 MW 20-yr ~$16B; Meta/Clinton 1.1 GW 20-yr) — high visibility but PPA-concentration rather than retail diversification.
- **NRG** is the *largest retail* book (~6M + Vivint 2.37M) but minimal nuclear — its "clean baseload" story is weak; it is a gas+retail+consumer-services play.
- **TLN** is essentially uncontracted merchant outside the Susquehanna-AWS 1,920 MW PPA (17-yr, ~$18B) — highest beta to power curves.

## 4. Why The Multiple Gap? — Decomposing VST's Discount to CEG

VST trades **~9.4x 2026E** vs CEG **~11.4x** — a **~2.0-turn (~21%) forward EV/EBITDA discount** (the headline/trailing and P/E gaps look larger: P/E 13.8x vs 21.4x ≈ 36% discount). Decomposition:

| Factor | Direction | Magnitude (qualitative) | Justified? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nuclear-purity premium** (CEG ~32.4 GW carbon-free baseload vs VST ~6.4 GW) | CEG premium | **Largest single driver.** Nuclear gets a "clean, 24/7, PPA-able, IRA-PTC-floored" premium; VST's gas weight caps its multiple | **Partly justified** — nuclear earnings are more durable/contractible; but VST's gas is also AI-levered and PJM-capacity-rich |
| **Merchant / commodity exposure** (VST higher gas + ERCOT spark-spread beta) | CEG premium | Moderate. VST EBITDA more sensitive to power/gas curves | **Justified** — genuine higher cash-flow variance |
| **Integration / acquisition risk** | Mixed | VST integrating Lotus + Cogentrix; **CEG integrating the much larger Calpine** (~$26.6B) | **Tilts toward VST** — CEG carries the *bigger* integration + FERC-divestiture (~3.5 GW) execution risk right now; market is under-pricing this |
| **Balance sheet** | CEG premium | VST net debt ~$19–20B vs CEG ~$17.5B total debt (but CEG EV-implied net debt ~$20.8B — similar). VST leverage ~2.5–3.0x; both investment-grade | **Overstated** — post-Calpine CEG is *not* the de-levered name it was; the leverage gap has largely closed |
| **Growth optics** | CEG premium | CEG's 60%+ 2026 EBITDA jump + "20% EPS CAGR" headline screens better than VST's +22% | **Partly mechanical** — CEG's growth is M&A-bought (Calpine), not organic; VST's is organic + accretive M&A. Quality of growth favors VST more than the multiple implies |
| **Integrated retail hedge** (VST ~5M customers) | Should *narrow* gap | VST's retail short lowers cash-flow volatility — a *quality* attribute the market under-credits | **Mispricing in VST's favor** |

**Verdict on the gap:** **Partly justified, partly mispricing.** The portion attributable to *nuclear purity + commodity beta* (~1.0–1.3 turns) is defensible. The portion attributable to *balance-sheet* and *growth-optics* (~0.7–1.0 turns) is stale or mechanical: post-Calpine CEG's leverage and integration risk have converged toward VST's, while VST's retail hedge and higher FCF yield (7.2% vs 3.8%) are quality offsets the market under-weights. **Net: VST's ~21% forward discount is wider than fundamentals justify by perhaps ~0.5–1.0 turn — a modest mispricing in VST's favor**, though not an egregious one given CEG's genuine nuclear-scarcity premium.

## 5. Pair-Trade Framing — Long VST / Short CEG vs Inverse

**Spread (June 9 2026):** Forward EV/EBITDA: VST 9.4x vs CEG 11.4x → **gap ~2.0 turns / VST at ~82% of CEG's multiple**. P/E: 13.8x vs 21.4x. FCF yield: 7.2% vs 3.8% (VST +340 bps).

**Correlation/beta:** Both are AI-power / merchant-IPP names with high pairwise correlation (~0.7–0.85 typical for the sub-sector; same demand thesis, same ISO exposure overlap in PJM/ERCOT, same rate/factor sensitivity). A long/short isolates the *relative* nuclear-purity-vs-integrated-merchant view while hedging the common AI-power-demand beta — a clean relative-value structure.

### Leg A — LONG VST / SHORT CEG (valuation convergence)
- **Thesis:** ~2-turn discount over-penalizes VST for gas/merchant exposure and stale balance-sheet optics; post-Calpine CEG now carries the *larger* integration + FERC-divestiture risk and has de-rated (CEG −16% LTM, down 13% YTD into Q1). VST's retail hedge + 7.2% FCF yield + organic-plus-accretive growth should re-rate the gap toward ~1.0–1.5 turns.
- **What makes it work:** (1) Calpine integration friction / FERC divestiture drag at CEG; (2) ERCOT/PJM power & capacity strength flowing to VST's merchant length; (3) VST PPA conversions (AWS Q4'27 ramp, Meta) de-risking the merchant tag; (4) continued VST buyback shrinking the float.
- **Risks:** nuclear-scarcity premium *widens* (new hyperscaler nuclear PPAs land at CEG); a gas/power-price down-cycle hits VST's merchant book harder.

### Leg B — SHORT VST / LONG CEG (nuclear-purity + scale premium)
- **Thesis:** Nuclear is the scarce, contractible, AI-preferred asset; CEG's ~32.4 GW is un-replicable and the premium should *persist or widen* as more 20-yr hyperscaler nuclear PPAs print. VST's gas-heavy book faces commodity normalization and IRA/PTC is nuclear-favored.
- **What makes it work:** new CEG hyperscaler nuclear deals; Calpine synergies beating plan; power-price normalization compressing VST merchant EBITDA.
- **Risks:** CEG's 21x forward P/E leaves little margin for integration slips; VST's FCF yield + buyback is a strong floor.

### **Verdict: Leg A (LONG VST / SHORT CEG) is the better risk/reward at current prices.**
The convergence trade is favored because (1) the discount is ~0.5–1.0 turn wider than fundamentals justify (Section 4); (2) the historical rationale for CEG's premium — *lower leverage and lower integration risk* — has **inverted** post-Calpine (CEG now has the bigger deal to digest and a forced divestiture); (3) VST offers a materially higher FCF yield (7.2% vs 3.8%, +340 bps of carry that *pays you to be long the cheap leg*), and the short CEG leg has negative carry of only ~0.68% dividend; (4) VST's catalyst path (PPA conversions, buyback, capacity prices) is more concrete near-term than incremental CEG nuclear deals. **Conviction: moderate, not high** — sized small because the common AI-power beta is strong and a single large CEG nuclear PPA headline could re-widen the spread against the trade. Target: gap compression from ~2.0 turns toward ~1.0–1.5 turns; stop if gap widens beyond ~2.7 turns on a CEG nuclear-PPA catalyst.
