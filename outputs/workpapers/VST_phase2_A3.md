## 1. PPA / Data-Center Pipeline BEYOND AWS + Meta

### 1a. The headline contractable figure: ~3.2 GW incremental nuclear

Both the **Q4'25 call (Feb 26 2026)** and **Q1'26 call (May 2026)** quantify the *same* near-term contracting prize:

> CFO K. Moldovan (Q1'26): "Our near-term priorities include **approximately 3.2 gigawatts of nuclear capacity at Beaver Valley and Comanche Peak that can be contracted on a long-term basis**." **(S1, Q1'26 transcript)**

> CEO J. Burke (Q4'25): "We still see an opportunity to contract **up to an additional 3.2 gigawatts of nuclear capacity** across our Beaver Valley and Comanche Peak sites, **including potential upgrades of approximately 200 megawatts at Comanche Peak**." **(S1, Q4'25 transcript, Motley Fool)**

**Interpretation:** the 3.2 GW is *fleet-wide* uncontracted nuclear (Beaver Valley remaining + Comanche Peak Unit 2 + uprates), NOT just Comanche Peak. Luminix flags this explicitly: "appears to reference fleet-wide availability, not just Comanche Peak unit 2" **(S5, Luminix Apr 2026)**.

### 1b. Comanche Peak — half the plant still uncontracted

- Comanche Peak total nameplate **~2,400 MW (2 units)** **(S5, Luminix Apr 2026, modeled)**.
- AWS contracted **1,200 MW** → **~1,200 MW (Unit 2 equivalent) remains uncontracted** **(S1 8-K; S5 Luminix)**.
- Management: ERCOT cited as fastest-interconnect / "speed-to-market" advantage; Comanche Peak is the most-shopped Texas asset **(S5, Utility Dive; power-eng)**.
- Comanche Peak uprate opportunity: **>200 MW** **(S1, both transcripts)**.

### 1c. Gas-for-AI / co-location / brownfield — qualitative but real

This is the **second leg** beyond nuclear. Management has been explicit it is NOT nuclear-only:

> Burke (Q1'26): "We do think there's **opportunity to do additional deals like the one we did with Meta. Doesn't need to just be with nuclear. I think we have opportunities to do it with gas as well**." **(S1, Q1'26 transcript)**

> S. Dore, Pres. Vistra Zero (Q4'25): "We continue to have a **high level of interest from customers on all of these different arrangements, including colocation with existing [and] new build**." **(S1, Q4'25 transcript)**

- **Two large counterparties** in discussion about **new-build gas plants** to support DC projects (PJM + ERCOT interest); separate early hyperscaler talks on **nuclear uprates** — first disclosed Q3'24, framing persists **(S5, power-eng / powermag Nov 2024; S1 2025–26 transcripts)**.
- Disclosed gas/brownfield development buckets: **Texas gas expansions / plant augmentations, Permian new-build gas units, coal-to-gas conversions at Coleto Creek and Miami Fort**, plus ~**300 MW** of PJM gas-site uprates **(S1, Q1'26 transcript, slide 7)**.
- **Cogentrix (~5,496 MW gas, ~$4B, closing mid-late '26)** is the strategic enabler: positions VST in supply-constrained PJM/ISO-NE for gas bridge-power deals **(S1, 8-K Feb 2026; S5 Enverus Jan 22 2026)**.

### 1d. Organic development pipeline

**~4,500 MW organic pipeline** (renewables + thermal expansions + nuclear uprates), most online **by 2028** **(S1, Q1'26 transcript; S5 Enverus)**.

### 1e. The dollar-quantified next leg

> Moldovan (Q4'25): "Contract economics for **recent Meta and Cogentrix transactions could add $700 million–$750 million in annual opportunity**." **(S1, Q4'25 transcript)**

> Management: nuclear PPAs (AWS + Meta + path) create "a pathway to **nearly 25% adjusted FCF-before-growth accretion on an annual basis**." **(S1, Q4'25 transcript)**

### PIPELINE VERDICT — Section 1
**Yes, a credible next leg of contracted MW exists, but it is OPTIONS not BACKLOG.** The hard, quantified, IG-signed number remains **3,809 MW** (AWS+Meta). Beyond that: **~3.2 GW uncontracted nuclear** (the clearest, highest-quality next tranche — Comanche Peak Unit 2 ~1,200 MW + Beaver Valley remainder + ~200 MW uprates) plus an **unquantified gas/co-location pipeline** (≥2 named-but-anonymous gas counterparties, "high level of interest"). **Zero new signed MW between Meta (Jan'26) and the Q1'26 call (May'26)** — management repeatedly declined to give LOI/MOU counts or MW. So the next leg is real and management-corroborated, but as of mid-2026 it is **conversations, not paper**. Conversion is the swing variable for the bull case.

Hyperscalers self-describe as **supply-constrained, not demand-constrained**. By May 2026 **every major hyperscaler has ≥1 nuclear deal; 13 projects = >9.8 GW nuclear committed** **(S5, smrintel May 2026)**.

### 2b. The digestion episode — early 2025, now lapped

- **Microsoft (Feb–Mar 2025):** TD Cowen channel checks flagged Microsoft canceling/walking from leases — "couple hundred MW" with ≥2 operators initially, broadening to **~2 GW** of US/Europe projects; attributed to AI-cluster oversupply + using power delays as cover **(S5, DCD / Bloomberg / TD Cowen Feb–Mar 2025)**.
- **Resolution:** by 2026 this reversed — Microsoft 2026 capex guide $110–120B (up), $80B unfillable Azure backlog. The 2025 pullback looks like a *re-allocation* (intl→US) and lease-vs-build optimization, **not structural demand destruction**.
- **Construction-side digestion is real but it's a SUPPLY problem:** of ~12 GW DC demand announced for 2026 completion, **only ~1/3 has broken ground**; ~$150–200B of spend pushed into 2027–28 **(S5, tech-insider 2026)**. This *delays* but does not cancel load — and favors firm-power offtakers like VST.
- **No PPA cancellations/renegotiations on firm nuclear deals.** Industry firm-nuclear PPAs (Meta-Vistra Jan'26, Google-TotalEnergies GW-scale Feb'26) are NEW signings, not cancellations **(S5, enkiai / smrintel 2026)**. The one notable *restructuring* (Talen/AWS Susquehanna, moved from co-location to FTM Jun'25) was driven by FERC structure, not demand **(S5, 2025; Phase 1 A1)**.

| Counterparty | MW | % of signed PPA MW | Moody's | S&P | Fitch | S-tag |

- **Both solidly IG**, in fact **high-IG (AA-band S&P / A1–Aa3 Moody's)** — among the strongest corporate credits globally. Counterparty default risk on the 20-yr cash flows is de minimis.
- **Concentration:** only 2 counterparties for 3,809 MW of multi-decade contracted nuclear. **Meta ~69%, AWS ~31%.** Single-name (Meta) concentration is the relevant tail; mitigants = Aa3/AA- credit, 20-yr term, license-extension funding aligns Meta's incentives. The ~5M retail book (Sec 4) is the structural diversifier vs. a pure-merchant or pure-PPA peer.

### DURABILITY VERDICT — Section 2
**Demand is still accelerating into mid-2026, not digesting.** Hyperscaler 2026 capex +60–77% YoY; supply-constrained not demand-constrained; $80B unfillable backlog at Microsoft alone. The early-2025 Microsoft pullback has been lapped and reversed. The only "digestion" is construction/interconnection *slippage* (~2/3 of 2026 demand un-broken-ground), which delays but does not destroy load and structurally favors VST's firm-power positioning. No firm-nuclear PPA cancellations industry-wide. Counterparties are top-decile IG; Meta single-name concentration (~69%) is the one tail to monitor.

### 3c. Premium-vs-merchant assessment

- **Merchant reference:** EIA expects 2026 load-weighted wholesale across 11 regions **~$51/MWh**; ERCOT North wholesale forecast **+45% in 2026** (off a low base) **(S5, EIA STEO / Utility Dive 2026)**. ERCOT North merchant ATC realistically low-$50s to ~$60/MWh.
- **PPA estimate ~$110/MWh** → **roughly 2x merchant = clear PREMIUM** **(S5, Luminix, modeled — NOT disclosed by VST)**. Economically rational: hyperscaler pays a premium for 24/7 carbon-free firm baseload + tenor certainty + relicensing assurance; VST converts merchant/recontracting risk into IG-contracted 20-yr cash flow.
- **Escalators / take-or-pay:** NOT disclosed (NDA). Structure is "up to 1,200 MW" with **8–10% FCFbG accretion contingent on full-capacity utilization** — implies a **volume/utilization-linked** rather than pure unconditional take-or-pay; full economics require the customer to take full volume. This is the key economic caveat: headline accretion is a *full-utilization* figure.

### 3d. Verifying the ~8–10% FCFbG accretion math

- FY25 Ongoing Adj FCFbG = **$3.592B** **(S1/S5)**. 8–10% ≈ **$287–359M** incremental annual FCFbG from AWS at full ramp.
- Cross-check via price: 1,200 MW × ~90% nuclear CF × 8,760 h ≈ **9.46 TWh/yr**. At an estimated ~$110/MWh that is **~$1.04B gross revenue**; the *incremental* vs. selling the same MWh merchant (~$55/MWh ≈ $0.52B) is a **~$520M gross uplift**, which after the fact that this output was already being produced/sold merchant (so incremental FCF ≈ the price *spread* minus modest costs) lands in the **~$300–360M** zone. **The ~8–10% (~$290–360M) accretion is internally consistent** with a ~2x-merchant price on ~9.5 TWh, *conditional on full 1,200 MW utilization and the merchant-price counterfactual* **(derived; price input S5-modeled, so treat the point estimate as indicative not precise)**.

### ECONOMICS VERDICT — Section 3
PPAs are a **premium to merchant (~2x, est. ~$110/MWh vs low-$50s ERCOT)** — VST is monetizing scarcity of firm carbon-free baseload. AWS structure is the *better* one (front-of-meter ERCOT, FERC-immune, customer-supplied backup). The ~8–10% FCFbG accretion is verified as internally consistent but is a **full-utilization** number and rests on **S5-estimated pricing** (VST has not disclosed $/MWh, escalators, or take-or-pay terms). Treat exact $/MWh as modeled, not primary.

### RETAIL VERDICT — Section 4
Retail is a **high-quality, counter-cyclical hedge that over-earned in 2025 and is guided to normalize ~14% lower** (record $1.622B → ~$1.4B medium-term). Customer count growing/stable at ~5M; margins strong but mean-reverting as Energy Harbor + supply-cost tailwinds fade. The integrated retail/gen hedge (100% load matched) remains the structural differentiator vs. pure-play merchant/PPA peers. Watch: the FY25→FY26 retail EBITDA step-down is a modeled headwind, not a surprise.

## 5. Commercial Execution Risk

| Risk vector | Assessment | S-tag |
|---|---|---|
| **AWS Q4'27 in-service slippage** | No disclosed slippage as of Q1'26; on schedule (Q4'27 start, full 2032). Front-of-meter ERCOT delivery = **avoids the interconnection/FERC risk** that hit Talen/Susquehanna co-location. Lower execution risk than co-located peers. | **S1**, transcripts; **S5** structure reads |
| **Meta delivery** | Perry Dec'26 (operating asset, low build risk); Davis-Besse Dec'27; **uprates phased to 2034** — the 433 MW uprate tranche carries multi-year NRC/construction execution risk (9-yr uprate program, ~3,000 jobs). | **S1**, IR Jan'26 |
| **Interconnection** | Comanche Peak/ERCOT = fastest US interconnect, key reason it's the most-shopped asset. PJM (Beaver Valley) gated on **regulatory clarity** — Dore: rule-change "clarity and transparency...will facilitate getting a deal done at Beaver Valley" → next PJM deal is **rule-dependent**. | **S5** Utility Dive; **S1** Q4'25 |
| **Counterparty performance** | AWS/Meta top-decile IG (Sec 2c); minimal performance risk. | **S5** |
| **New-build gas/Cogentrix** | Cogentrix close mid-late'26 (regulatory); new-build gas + Permian units = multi-year construction; "new construction takes time" (Dore). Pipeline conversion is the gating risk, not counterparty. | **S1**, 8-K / transcript |
| **Uprate execution** | ~200 MW Comanche Peak + 433 MW Meta uprates + ~300 MW PJM gas uprates = NRC-permitted, multi-year, capex-heavy; slippage risk on timing/cost. | **S1**, transcripts |

### EXECUTION VERDICT — Section 5
**Signed deals (AWS, Meta operating MW) are low-risk on counterparty and, for AWS, structurally de-risked (front-of-meter ERCOT, customer backup).** The execution risk concentrates in (a) the **uprate/new-build tranches** (Meta 433 MW to 2034, Comanche/PJM uprates, new gas) which carry NRC/construction/cost-overrun timing risk, and (b) **PJM/Beaver Valley contracting gated on PJM co-location rule clarity**. No disclosed slippage on AWS Q4'27 as of Q1'26.
